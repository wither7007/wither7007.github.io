Bitter cold

I had been out of work for over a year, living on unemployment and a severance package. I had been drinking for a year straight, now I was running out of health and money. The rent was over due.

I went to get some liquor one night. When I returned, I heard the voices of my landlord, my ex-girlfriend and another friend coming from my apartment. I was walking into an intervention. I have no idea what I was thinking, but I zipped my coat and walked into a very cold winter.

I spent that night in the U of M Library across the street, drinking my rum with a Coke, sleeping in the stacks. The next morning I knew I was fucked. I had worked at the University for 20 years, familiar with the sleeping spots, the free food, the open showers; but now homeless, it was all different and sinister. It took a couple days but eventually someone called the campus police. I had an outstanding DWI and was hauled to jail. I suffered an alcohol withdrawal seizure while in jail and spent a couple days in County Hospital, getting some badly needed magnesium and other salts. Back in jail I told another inmate about my situation and wondered if I could stay in jail until I figured something out. He correctly told me, being a first offense, they would cut me loose in the morning. He also mentioned something about a place called St. Stephens. I left jail and wandered around the city for a couple days, taking too much note of rail heights on the overpasses. I finally ended up in the library and began researching St. Stephen. After a couple hours searching I was able to locate the church, they told me to go to Cental Lutheran Church and talk to an outreach worker. I was setup with a visiting psychologist there who gave me a note saying I was a crazy alcoholic and sent across the street to request benefits . I went back to St. Stephens that night and finally found a bed. I stayed at St. Stephens for a couple wickedly cold nights, and Monday went to Simpsons shelter where I was entered into a "lottery". I won and was granted a 30 day reprieve from the winter.

Jim Steffes
