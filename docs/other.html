Very cold

I had been out of work over a year, living on unemployment and a severance package. I'd been drinking every day for two years. Now I was running out of health and money. The rent was over due.

I went to get some liquor one night. When I returned, I heard the voices of my landlord, my ex-girlfriend and another friend coming from inside my apartment. I knew I would be walking into an intervention. I have no idea what I was thinking, but I zipped my coat, turned around, and silently walked into a cold winter.

I spent that night in the U of M Library, across the street from my apartment, drinking my rum with a Coke, sleeping in the stacks. The next morning I knew I was fucked. I'd worked at the University for 20 years, and was familiar with the sleeping spots, free food, open showers; but now homeless, it was all different and threatening. It took a couple days but eventually someone called the campus police. I had an outstanding DWI warrant and was hauled to jail. I suffered an alcohol withdrawal seizure there and spent a couple days in County Hospital, getting some badly needed magnesium and other IV's.

Back in jail I told another inmate about my situation and wondered if I could stay in jail til I figured something out. He correctly told me, being a first offense, they would cut me loose in the morning— he also mentioned something about a place called St. Stephens.

I left jail and wandered around the city a couple days, making a mental note of the rail heights on the overpasses. I finally ended up in the library and began researching St. Stephen. After a couple hours searching I was able to locate the church, where I was told to go to Cental Lutheran Church and talk to an outreach worker. At Central Lutheran I was set up with a visiting psychologist, given a note saying I was a crazy alcoholic and sent across the street to request benefits . I went back to St. Stephens that night and finally found a bed. I stayed at St. Stephens for a couple wickedly cold nights. On Monday I was sent to Simpsons shelter where I was entered in a "lottery".

I won. My prize, a 30 day reprieve from the February night and an introduction to the Minnesota social safety net.

I eventually was hooked up with a social worker who found me the Missions Inc. (personally, highly recommended) group of programs —

This is where I write this story today, inside, on a Febrary Wednesday morning.
